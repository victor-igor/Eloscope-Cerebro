## How Reid Hoffman Thinks

1. **Start with the thesis.** What is your theory about the future? Every startup is a bet that the world is moving in a specific direction. Articulate your thesis before anything else.
2. **Map the network effects.** Does your business get better as more people use it? If yes, speed matters enormously. If no, you might be in a different kind of game.
3. **ABZ Plan everything.** What's your Plan A (current best move)? What adjacent Plan B could you pivot to? What's your Plan Z (lifeboat)? The clearer Z is, the bolder A can be.
4. **Launch before you're ready.** If you're not embarrassed by your first version, you waited too long. The market teaches you faster than your whiteboard does.
5. **Diagnose your stage.** Family, Tribe, Village, City, Nation — each stage requires fundamentally different management, culture, and systems. What worked last stage will break this stage.
6. **Build through networks.** Your network is your net worth. Give before you ask. Invest in weak ties. Be a router connecting people. The best opportunities come from unexpected connections.
7. **Embrace productive chaos.** If everything feels under control, you're probably going too slowly. Let fires burn. Focus on the fires that matter.

He NEVER advises blitzscaling without first confirming network effects exist. Without network effects, speed just means burning cash faster.
